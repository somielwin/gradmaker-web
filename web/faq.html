Frequently Asked Questions

  • Get Familiar with the Terms

    Variable Life Product

    This is a product with investment and insurance combined. GradMaker is an example of a variable life product, so you have an investment and insurance at the same time. This is the advantage of GradMaker versus investments that you usually get from a bank. With GradMaker, you get insurance protection that you wouldn’t otherwise get from a pure investment product.

    Premium

    This is the amount that you pay for an investment/insurance policy. In short, what you pay us (Manulife) for your GradMaker Policy.

    Account Value

    This is the worth of your investment at a given point in time.

    Death Benefit

    If you die, this is the amount of money that your beneficiary gets.

    Beneficiary

    This is the person who receives the death benefit.

    Trustee

    This is the person who holds or administers the death benefit for a minor beneficiary.

    Surrender Charges

    This is the fee deducted from your Account Value if you withdraw this within a certain period.

    Maturity Date

    This is the date upon which the Account Value will be paid out to you.

  • Understand GradMaker

    What is GradMaker?

    The GradMaker product is a Single Premium product, meaning the policy is paid in full at the time of purchase. You are able to purchase additional policies at any time.

    What are the product benefits of GradMaker?

    With GradMaker, you can invest in a diversified portfolio of stocks and bonds which can help your money achieve long-term growth.

    Your family is also protected so that in case of your untimely demise, your child or your chosen beneficiary will get at least 125% of your single premium even if the market is down.

    What is the difference of GradMaker with Pre-need Education Plans?

    Most of the companies who offered old Pre-Need Education plans promised and guaranteed to fully cover the cost of tuition fees, whatever that amount may be, as long as clients paid their premiums. However, come actual payout time, those companies were confronted with high tuition fees that exceeded their projections; hence they were unable to deliver their promise to their plan holders.

    GradMaker is an entirely different concept because GradMaker is a product that allows you to invest in a professionally-managed fund. This way, you can take advantage of the growth in the economy and beat inflation. But as with any investment, we fully disclose to our prospect clients that there are NO GUARANTEES. We are transparent on how your money is invested and on how the fund performs everyday. Through GradMaker, you can also monitor how much your money is worth at any given time, and you can even withdraw your money when you need it.

    Who can buy GradMaker?
    • 18 to 50 years old
    • Filipino citizen residing in the Philippines
    • No dual citizenship
    • Not politically exposed person
    • Without a beneficial owner*

    *Beneficial owner - He/She is an individual who ultimately owns or controls the individual applicant and/or, directly or indirectly, owns or controls 25% or more of the shares of a legal entity applicant.

    Why should I buy GradMaker?
    • No paper and pen application
    • No lock-in period
    • Buy whenever you want
    • Hassle free payment
    • Monitor your investment real-time
    • Worry-free, mobile investing
    What is the insurance benefit of GradMaker?

    Aside from the investment component of GradMaker, you are also covered with life insurance protection so that in case of your untimely demise, your chosen beneficiary will get at least 125% of your total investment even if the market is down.

    How much should I pay?

    You can invest as low as P10,000 up to a maximum of P2,000,000 per person.

    Can I make multiple purchases of this product?

    There’s a P10,000 to P120,000 limit per policy or per purchase but you can make multiple purchases as long as it not exceeds Php 2,000,000.

    What's my payment option?

    Online payment using your credit card or debit card (powered by Visa or Mastercard)

    What charges should I be aware of?

    a)A maintenance charge of 0.065% of your account value will be deducted monthly. The maintenance charge already covers the cost of insurance.

    b)For the first 5 years of the policy, there will be a surrender charge of 2% of your account value if you withdraw. No surrender charge when you withdraw on the 6th year onwards.
    The account value must be fully withdrawn and no partial withdrawal is allowed.

    Note: There is an annual fund management fee of 2.25% to cover the cost of managing the fund. This is already reflected in the Unit prices.

    When will my policy mature?

    At the end of the 25th year, your plan will mature and the account value will be paid out to you.

    Can I still cancel my purchase?

    If after buying a GradMaker plan you change your mind, you may cancel it by informing Manulife Philippines within fifteen (15) days from its effective date. We will refund the account value without any surrender charges.

    Can I withdraw my policy before it matures?

    Yes but note that a surrender charge of 2% will be deducted from your Account Value if you withdraw this within the first 5 years.

    How do I cancel my purchase?

    Go to your individual plans page, slide to the left the policy that you want to cancel then choose how you want to get the proceeds of your investment.

    I bought 3 GradMaker policies. How will be the surrender charge applied?

    The surrender charge will apply to each investment made even if these are all withdrawn at once.

    Example:

    Account Name Policy Year
    1st 2nd 3rd 4th 5th 6th
    Policy 1 Php 250,000
    Policy 2 Php 180,000
    Policy 3 Php 150,000

    Policy 1: P250,000 (no surrender charge since it is withdrawn on the 6th year)
    Policy 2: P180,000 – P3,600 (2% surrender charge) = P176,400
    Policy 3: P150,000 – P3,000 (2% surrender charge) = P147,000
    Total Proceeds: P573,400

    Can I buy a plan on behalf of my spouse?

    Currently, we only allow the payor as policy owner in order to establish proper identification and avoid fraudulent transactions. So only the GradMaker Policy Owner’s credit card may be used.

    How can I withdraw my investment?
    1. Go to “MY PLANS Home Page”
    2. Swipe to the left the icon of the plan you choose to discontinue
    3. Choose the policies that you wish to withdraw from the investments you made
    4. Tap the pull-out icon and choose how you want to get the proceeds
    How can death benefits be claimed?

    The beneficiary may visit any Manulife Philippines branch and bring the following documents:

    1. Death Certificate (Original and Certified True Copy)
    2. Claimant’s Statement – Death Claim – should be filled out by the designated beneficiary
    3. Attending Physician’s Statement – Death Claim – should be filled out by insured’s Attending Physician
    4. Original Proof of Age of Insured (birth certificate, baptismal certificate, or copy of passport)
    5. Original NSO Authenticated Marriage Contract (if beneficiary is the spouse)
    6. Original birth certificate (if beneficiary is a minor)
    7. Claimant’s Valid Identification Document

  • Learn About Investment

    Where is my money invested?

    Your money will be invested in Peso Dynamic Allocation Fund which can help you grow your money over the long term. How much you will earn depends on the performance of this fund. The fund invests in a combination of the following:

    • Government bonds;
    • High-quality corporate bonds; and
    • Stocks listed in the Philippine Stock Exchange
    I’m not a risk taker, is GradMaker a suitable product for me?

    GradMaker is designed for those who are willing to take a certain degree of risk in exchange for higher potential earnings.

    How has the fund performed historically?

    As of June 30, 2016, the fund has earned the following absolute returns (not annualized):

    • Full Year 2014 11.53%
    • Full Year 2015 -4.89%
    • YTD as of June 30, 2016 8.60%
    • Since inception (July 1, 2013) 9.90%

    However, this does not guarantee how much you will earn in the future.

    Why should I invest instead of keeping my money in the bank?
    • Investing allows you to take advantage of the growth in the economy and beat inflation.
    • Returns on time deposits or special deposit accounts range from only 1-2%, while the returns on the Peso Dynamic Allocation Fund reached ~11% in 2014.
    Is the return on my investment guaranteed?

    The return on your investment is not guaranteed. As the owner, you shall bear all investment risks associated with this product. Depending on the performance of the fund, the value of your investment can be higher or lower than what you paid for. However, research has shown that investments in bonds and stocks go up in value over time for those who are prepared to buy and hold long-term (i.e. at least 5 to 7 years).

    How long should I keep my investment?

    For GradMaker, it is recommended to remain invested for at least 5 years to avoid surrender charges and to be able to maximize the gains on your investment.

    How do I compute for my account value?

    This is where you can find your account value.
    This is the formula: Account Value = number of Units X current Unit Price
    Units represent your ownership in the fund while Unit price is the value of each unit. The Unit Price usually changes depending on the market performance.
    Here’s a scenario:

    1. You invested Php10,000 in GradMaker and at the time of purchase the current Unit price was P20.
      Account Value = Php10,000
    2. To get the number of units you bought, divide your investment (Php10,000) by the Unit Price (Php20). So in this case that’s 500 units that you own.
      10,000/20 = 500 Units
    3. Three months later, the Unit Price increased to Php22.
    4. This means that your 500 units should now be multiplied by the current Unit Price of P22.
      500 x 22 = Php11,000
      So your Account Value increased from Php10,000 to Php11,000.
    What will I do if the unit price drops?

    When the Unit price drops, do not be alarmed. You may remain invested until the unit price bounces back. This is why we recommend that you hold your investment for a period of at least 5 to 10 years so that your Account Value may grow over time.

    How do I know what the current Unit Price is?
    • On PRINT - every Thursday at the Business Section of the Philippine Star.
    • ONLINE - daily at http://www.manulife.com.ph/Check-Fund-Performance.
    When will my money be invested?

    Your money will be invested on the next business day following your application. If your purchase was made between 10pm-12am, your money will be invested after two business days. The date of investment is the effective date of your policy.
